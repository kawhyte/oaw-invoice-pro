# OW Studio — What's New: Sharing Drawings & Getting Paid

A quick guide to the new way of sending work to clients, controlling what they see, and getting the final files unlocked only after payment.

---

## The big idea

You now have **two separate places** to put files on a project, and they behave very differently:

| | **Documents** | **Drawings** |
|---|---|---|
| What it's for | Contracts, permits, invoices, reference files | Your actual architectural deliverable (ArchiCAD sheets) |
| What the client gets | The real file, downloadable right away | A **watermarked, low-res preview** to review… |
| Payment | Not tied to payment | …and the **print-ready file unlocks only when the invoice is paid** |
| Your control | A switch on each file to share it or keep it private | A switch to release the final early if you choose |

**Rule of thumb:** if it's the **paid work product**, it's a **Drawing**. If it's a contract, permit, reference file, or invoice, it's a **Document**.

---

## 1. Drawings — the draft → paid → final flow

This is the part that fixes "clients take the file without paying."

**How you use it:**
1. On a project, go to the **Drawings** section → **+ Add drawing**.
2. Pick your big ArchiCAD PDF (exported as normal).
3. Optionally link it to an invoice — e.g. *"Unlock when INV-202 is paid."*
4. Tap **Upload drawing**. Your phone prepares the preview (you'll see "Preparing preview 3/8…"). That's it.

**What the client sees on the share link:**
- A **watermarked, low-resolution preview** of every page — they can read it and request changes, but it's stamped "DRAFT — NOT FOR PRINT" and is **useless for printing**.
- They can **tap to zoom** and pinch on their phone to inspect detail.
- The **"Download print-ready file"** button is **locked** until either:
  - the linked invoice is marked **paid**, or
  - you flip the **"Release to client"** switch yourself.

**Why this helps:**
- The client can review and ask for changes, but can't take a usable file until they pay.
- It loads fast even on slow internet (see below).
- It keeps your revision rounds honest — they're reviewing a proof, not collecting the final.

---

## 2. Why the draft is an image, not a PDF

This is on purpose. The draft a client reviews is a set of **images**, while the final they download is the real **PDF**. Five reasons:

1. **Speed on slow internet.** Each page is a small (~150 KB) image that loads one at a time, instead of a big multi-megabyte PDF that has to fully download before it even opens. This matters most for clients on weak connections.
2. **It can't be printed or used without paying.** A low-resolution image can't be plotted at print quality, so nobody can take the draft and build from it.
3. **It works on any phone instantly.** Images open natively with zoom; PDFs on phones are clunky (download prompts, separate apps).
4. **The watermark can't be removed.** It's baked into the picture, not a layer that can be stripped out.
5. **You don't lose anything.** The real print-ready PDF is kept safe as the *final* — it just stays locked until payment. Best of both worlds: a fast, safe image to review, and the true PDF to deliver.

## 3. Documents — supporting files

The **Documents** section is for everything that isn't the drawing deliverable. **It accepts PDF files only** — so scan or export anything else (a photo, a Word contract) to PDF first. Good examples:
- Signed **contract / proposal / agreement** (PDF)
- **Permits, approvals, regulatory letters** (PDF)
- The **invoice PDF** (from "Save for Client")
- **Reference material as PDF** — site survey, soil report, the client's existing plans

**New: you control each file.** Every file now has a **"Visible to client"** switch.
- **On** (default) → the client can see and download it on the share link.
- **Off** → it stays private to you (handy for internal markups, or an invoice PDF you don't want shared).

---

## 4. Knowing exactly what your client sees

Every section on the project page now has a clear label:

- 🟢 **Client can view** — Documents (the visible ones), Progress Notes
- 🟠 **Final unlocks when paid** — Drawings
- ⚪ **Private · only you** — Invoices, Checklist

And at the bottom, the **Client Share Link** box has a **"What your client will see"** summary that lists Documents, Drawings, Progress notes, and (if you turn it on) the Invoice summary — so you can always double-check before sending the link.

**What the client never sees:** your Checklist and your Invoices list. Those are private to you. (You can optionally share a payment *summary* with the toggle in the Client Share Link box.)

---

## 5. The everyday workflow, start to finish

1. Create the project and client.
2. Put contracts/permits/reference files in **Documents** (mark any private if needed).
3. Export your drawings from ArchiCAD and upload them under **Drawings**, linked to the invoice.
4. Send the client the **share link** (Copy button in Client Share Link).
5. The client reviews the **watermarked draft**, requests changes; you re-upload as needed.
6. When the invoice is **paid**, the **print-ready file unlocks automatically** — or you release it manually if you decide to.
7. The client downloads the clean PDF, saved with a clear name like *"Tracktown Villa - Working Drawings.pdf."*
